# Program Evaluation Rubric (Windows Host Analyst Training)

Use this rubric to evaluate an analyst at the end of the training program.

---

## 1. Technical Skills

| Area | Max Points | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Live System Triage | 20 | Identifies suspicious processes, persistence, and C2 under time pressure. |
| EVTX and Artifact Analysis | 20 | Comfortably navigates Security, Sysmon, PowerShell, TaskScheduler, and other logs. |
| Timeline Construction | 15 | Builds clear timelines from multiple data sources with minimal guidance. |
| Tool Proficiency | 15 | Uses a mix of native tools and DFIR utilities effectively (Sysinternals, EvtxECmd, Chainsaw, etc.). |

Subtotal: 70 points.

---

## 2. Analytical Skills

| Area | Max Points | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Hypothesis-Driven Investigation | 10 | Forms and tests hypotheses instead of randomly hunting. |
| Pattern Recognition | 10 | Recognizes common TTP patterns (credential dumping, lateral movement, ransomware, etc.). |
| Dealing with Uncertainty | 10 | Clearly communicates what is known vs. unknown and why. |

Subtotal: 30 points.

---

## 3. Communication

| Area | Max Points | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| Written Narratives | 15 | Clear, concise incident summaries understandable by technical and non-technical audiences. |
| Verbal Explanations | 10 | Can brief findings and answer follow-up questions logically. |
| Recommendations | 10 | Provides realistic, prioritized remediation and hardening steps. |

Subtotal: 35 points.

---

## 4. Overall Scoring

- Technical Skills: 70  
- Analytical Skills: 30  
- Communication: 35  

**Total: 135 points**

You can normalize this to a 100-point scale if desired.
